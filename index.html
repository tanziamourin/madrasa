Our Rich History

Our madrasa has a rich history dating back over 57 years, providing quality Islamic education and fostering a strong sense of community.

Learn More....

Gogaura Dakhil Madrasa is located in Gogaura village, under Ward No. 1, of the Chunarughat Upazila, Habiganj District. It was founded with the support of the villagers by the late Alhaj Abdul Hai Chowdhury, who was the founding member.

The late Alhaj Abuleis Chowdhury was a donor member, and the late Ataur Rahman Chowdhury and the late Syed Ershad Ali made significant contributions during the madrasa's establishment.

Later on, Alhaj Fakhrul Islam Chowdhury, along with his family members, donated 30 decimals of land in 2018, where the current building now stands. After the passing of the founding member, Alhaj Abdul Hai Chowdhury, Alhaj Fakhrul Islam Chowdhury donated land worth around 18 lakh taka for the madrasa, and he was later recognized as a founding member.

In addition, 82 decimals of land in Amir Pur Mouza were donated through Mr. Koddus Chowdhury and the late Syed Ershad Ali.

At the time of establishment, Maulana Robi Ullah was the first superintendent. Subsequently, Maulana Idris Ali, Maulana Mofiz Uddin, and Maulana Shamsul Haque held the role one after another.

Currently, Ali Mohammad Chowdhury is serving as the madrasa's superintendent. Appointed in 1994, he, along with the local community and teachers, worked to ensure quality education and constructed a two-story building with four rooms.

There is also a government-funded one-story building, but vertical expansion is urgently needed. Notably, the number of female students exceeds the number of male students.

Builder of the Madrasa

Chief Educator of the Madrasa

Ali Muhammad Chowdhury (Index No. 05487), born on 01-01-1970, joined the madrasa on 01-09-1998 as the superintendent. He has played a pivotal role in advancing the institution's mission of providing quality education. Along with the support of the local community and teachers, he worked tirelessly to improve the facilities, leading the construction of a two-story building with four classrooms. His dedication and leadership continue to contribute significantly to the development and success of the madrasa..
